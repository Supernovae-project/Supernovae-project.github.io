The "Zombie Star"Source is potentially a star which has had a supernova explosion more than once. Supernova iPTF14hls was observed in Sept., 2014; over the next two years it oscilated in brightness, which was both far longer than the normal 100 days of a Supernova and followed an erratic brightnes pattern. What made it even more interesting was that a Supernova was recorded in 1954 in the exact same location leading scientists to postulate it could have been the same star.Source One theory postulates that multiple supernovae could be possible if the initial star was so hot and massive it formed antimatter in its core leading to unstable and unpredictable explosive events.Source Another thought is that the event was a transfer of energy from the remaining neutron star's enormous magnetic field spinning at 1000 times per second.Source

In June, 2015, scientists working on the ASASSN Project spotted a luminous event, which was believed to be the brightest Supernova ever witnessed in recorded history. The event was 20 times more luminous than the entire Milky Way Galaxy. Source Nothing as bright had been recorded so scientists came up with different theories about how it could have occured. One such theory was that the event was not actually the Supernova's initial explosion but transfer of energy from the remaining neutron star's enormous magnetic field spinning at 1000 times per second.Source However, a new theory suggests that the event was caused by a tidal disruption when a very high mass star was torn apart by a supermassive black hole.Source

Betelgeuse is the nearest star in our sky likely to go supernova. It is already one of the brightest stars in the sky and its proximity and its age means that it could potentially explode in our lifetime. If it does it will be the brightest object during the night, perhaps even brighter than the moon according to some scientists, and it will be visible in that state for more than a year. Why can't we tell when it will go supernova? The main problem is that scientists do not have the potential currently to detect neutrinos at the distance Betelgeuse is from Earth.Source
